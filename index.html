About the Book

A Coffee Shop Love Story

Maya Chen, 29, runs a struggling specialty coffee shop in Portland. She's stubborn, sharp, and secretly romantic — runs on cold brew and bad decisions. When a caffeine-fueled midnight order accidentally triggers a 500-pound bean emergency, she has three weeks to sell the coffee before her loan defaults.

The worst part? The only person who can help is Leo Bianchi — acquisitions director at Bean & Leaf Coffee, the chain store trying to put her out of business. He's charming, understated, and hiding his own corporate crisis.

What starts as a reluctant partnership turns into something neither of them expected. As they navigate supply chains, viral TikTok rescues, mysterious bean lotteries, and the quiet magic of a neighborhood coffee shop, Maya and Leo discover that the best things in life — like the best cups of coffee — are rarely planned.

About the Author

Alex Rogers

Alex Rogers writes romantic comedies about coffee, community, and the kind of love that sneaks up on you when you're too busy arguing about grind sizes to notice.

She believes in indie bookstores, third-wave espresso, and the idea that the best decisions are usually the bad ones that turn out to be exactly right.

Coffee Rings and Bad Decisions is her debut novel.
