# Coffee Rings and Bad Decisions — Outline

## Premise

Maya Chen, 29, runs a struggling specialty coffee shop in Portland and accidentally orders 500 pounds of single-origin Ethiopian beans after a caffeine-fueled argument with a customer who turns out to be the new head of acquisitions for the chain store trying to put her out of business. Now she has 30 days to sell the beans before her loan defaults, and the only person who can help is the man she publicly called a "corporate suit with a caffeine allergy."

## Core Themes

- Second chances
- Career vs. love
- Digital detox / analog revival
- Found family / community over algorithms

## Characters

### Maya Chen
- 29, coffee shop owner
- Sharp, stubborn, secretly romantic
- Runs on cold brew and bad decisions
- Arc: learns to ask for help, stops fighting everything alone

### Leo Bianchi
- 32, acquisitions director at Bean & Leaf Co.
- Charming, understated, hates being the bad guy
- Grew up in a family restaurant, knows what losing a business feels like
- Arc: stops playing it safe, chooses passion over paycheck

### Side Characters

- **Sam** (28, Maya's best friend, barista): chaotic support, wingman energy, runs a side hustle selling vintage mugs
- **Mrs. Hsu** (72, regular): silent guardian angel, actually owns the building Maya leases
- **Jake** (27, Maya's ex): influencer boyfriend who tried to turn her shop into a content farm
- **Rachel** (31, Leo's boss): shark in a blazer, wants to acquire Maya's shop at any cost

## Act Structure

### Act 1 — The Mistake (Chapters 1-12)

**Chapter 1-3:** Establish Maya's world. Coffee shop chaos, loan pressure, her rivalry with Bean & Leaf. Meet-cute: Leo comes in for a coffee, they clash over pour-over technique and "big corporation" ethics. Maya has no idea he's the enemy.

**Chapter 4-6:** Maya receives the 500-lb bean delivery. Caffeine-fueled order confirmation email. She panics. Sam tries to sell "Mystery Bean Lottery" tickets.

**Chapter 7-9:** Maya discovers Leo's identity. Public confrontation at a local coffee festival. She calls him a "corporate suit" in front of industry press. Career suicide for both of them.

**Chapter 10-12:** Leo gets fired from Bean & Leaf for the incident. He shows up at Maya's shop, wet and unemployed, offering to help her sell the beans. She hates it. She says no. He shows up the next day anyway because her shop is the only place that makes him feel like home.

### Act 2 — The Partnership (Chapters 13-35)

**Chapter 13-20:** Reluctant collaboration. They discover they actually make a good team — Leo knows supply chains, Maya knows customers. Comedy beats: cupping disasters, Instagrammable latte art fails, a coffee-related food poisoning incident that turns out to be a PR win.

**Chapter 21-28:** Growing attraction under pressure. Late nights sorting beans, inside jokes about "acidity levels" and "roast profiles," a almost-kiss interrupted by a burst of coffee grounds. Sam and Mrs. Hsu watch from the sidelines with conspiracy-level plotting.

**Chapter 29-35:** Setback: Bean & Leaf offers Maya a buyout. Jake returns, begging for a second chance. Leo gets a job offer in Seattle. Both leads are pulled in opposite directions. Fight scene in the shop. Maya says she doesn't need anyone. Leo leaves without saying goodbye.

### Act 3 — The Brew (Chapters 36-45)

**Chapter 36-40:** Maya realizes the shop is empty without Leo. The bean sale completes, the loan is saved, but she's miserable. She figures out that Mrs. Hsu has been her guardian angel landlord all along.

**Chapter 41-44:** Grand gesture: Maya drives to Seattle, crashes Leo's new job interview, and declares that the real acquisition target is her heart. Witty dialogue. Emotional vulnerability. Leo quits the Seattle job and moves back.

**Chapter 45:** Epilogue. Coffee shop thriving. Leo as co-owner. Bean & Leaf out of the picture. A montage of coffee-related milestones. Final line about coffee rings and bad decisions being the best ones.

## Emotional Arcs

- **Maya:** Closed-off → Vulnerable. Her arc is learning to let people in, to accept help, to stop seeing independence as a wall.
- **Leo:** People-pleaser → Assertive. His arc is learning to choose himself, to stop doing what's safe, to fight for something that matters.

## Comedy Beats

- Coffee puns used sincerely and poorly
- "Coffee god" fantasy sequences
- Instagram vs. reality latte art wars
- The 500-lb bean avalanche
- Mrs. Hsu's passive-aggressive notes about noise complaints
- Sam's conspiracy theories about Bean & Leaf
- A disastrous blind cupping that turns into a viral TikTok

## Satisfying Resolution

- Beans sold, loan paid off, shop thriving
- Leo stays (no Seattle job)
- Maya doesn't sell, doesn't compromise
- Jake arc: he learns his lesson, becomes a real customer
- Bean & Leaf: redrawn as a smaller, more ethical buyer who actually cares about coffee
- No wedding. No baby. Just two people who chose each other while saving a coffee shop.
