# Chapter 14 — Static

The almost-kiss happened three more times during the next week.

It was always the same setup. Late night. The shop was empty or nearly empty. They were finishing up, wiping down the counter, counting the day's receipts. There was a lull. A pause. A moment where Maya looked up and Leo was looking at her, and the space between them felt charged, like someone had connected a live wire to the floorboards.

Each time, something intervened.

-Apter 14: hand sanitizer dispenser exploding when Leo bumped the counter.
-Apter 14.5: Mrs. Hsu walking in at 9 p.m. because she'd locked herself out of her apartment and needed to make a phone call.
-Apter 14.9: Sam texting Maya mid-lean with a photo of a cat wearing a tiny apron and the caption "URGENT."

Maya began to suspect the universe was actively conspiring to keep them apart. Leo did not seem to suspect a conspiracy; he seemed to find the interventions more humiliating each time.

"Destiny doesn't want us to kiss," he said, on the fourth near-miss.

"Destiny is a barista with bad timing," Maya said.

"Destiny is a cat in a tiny apron," Leo corrected.

He was right. The universe was, in fact, run by a cat. Maya was fairly certain of this.
