## Book Description (Amazon Blurb)

Maya Chen didn't believe in bad mornings.

She believed in bad beans, bad weather, and bad decisions made before 7 a.m., but mornings themselves were neutral territory. Then her caffeine-fueled midnight order accidentally triggered a fourteen-thousand-dollar coffee emergency — five hundred pounds of Ethiopian single-origin beans she couldn't afford, with three weeks to sell them before her loan defaults.

The worst part? The only person who can help is the man she publicly called a "corporate suit with a caffeine allergy" — Leo Bianchi, acquisitions director at Bean & Leaf Coffee, the chain store trying to put her out of business.

Now Maya and Leo are accidentally partners in a scheme that's equal parts brilliant and insane: sell the beans, save the shop, and try not to fall in love while doing it. Complicating matters: Leo's boss wants to acquire Maya's shop for parts, Maya's influencer ex-boyfriend keeps showing up with dramatic apologies, and the local coffee community has turned the whole thing into a viral rescue mission.

Warm, witty, and deeply romantic, Coffee Rings and Bad Decisions is the story of what happens when two stubborn people accidentally build something real — one cup at a time.
